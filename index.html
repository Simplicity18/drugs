Drug Inventory and Supply Chain Tracking System

To develop a real-time technology-driven drug inventory system that ensures stock visibility, prevents counterfeit circulation, reduces wastage, and guarantees uninterrupted healthcare delivery.

Problem Statement

Traditional drug inventory systems face mounting challenges: inaccurate stock records, lack of real-time visibility, and delayed replenishment cycles. The alarming rise of counterfeit medicines threatens patient safety. Manual processes lead to stockouts, wastage, and compromised healthcare delivery — emphasizing the urgent need for technology-driven solutions.

Market Research

The global drug inventory management system market is projected to grow from $6.4 billion in 2025 to over $10 billion by 2030, at a CAGR exceeding 9%. Growth is driven by reducing medication errors, managing expiration, and improving efficiency through digital transformation. Key trends include AI-driven forecasting, cloud-based systems, and automated dispensing cabinets. The market is dominated by centralized hospital systems, while independent pharmacies are rapidly adopting digital solutions. Major players include Omnicell, BD, and McKesson.

Research Gap / Innovation

Instead of relying on weekly or monthly manual inventory counts (periodic system), our system implements a perpetual inventory model that updates instantly as medications are sold, received, or dispensed — ensuring transparency, minimizing discrepancies, and improving supply chain efficiency.
